About Us

We are Chronically Healthy: a team of five biomedical engineering capstone students at the University of Toronto. Over the past year, we designed a practical solution to monitor the health of seniors with congestive heart failure (CHF).






About The Project

The team designed a system that helps seniors with CHF track their daily weight for signs of edema: fluid accumulation due to insufficient cardiac output. Rapid weight gain is an indicator of worsening heart condition in CHF patients. EdemAlert includes physical components to remind users to take their weight (color-changing light) and alert them to abnormal weight gains (color-changing light and phone call).

The team used IFTTT to link the website, scale, and light that is installed at the patient’s home. The light bulb will turn on everyday at the same time, based on the patients’ preference, to remind the patient to weigh themselves. Only after the weight of patients is recorded, will the light turn off. The weight data will be stored and processed by the website. When a potential heart failure trend is detected, the website will send a signal to the light bulb to display a red color. This will warn the patients to see the doctors. The algorithm for detecting unhealthy weight trends will be reset after each hospitalization, once the patient has sought out a physician’s advice.
